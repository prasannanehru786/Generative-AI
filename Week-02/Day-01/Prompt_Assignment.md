# RTCFR Prompt – Healthy South Indian Meal Planner

## Role
You are a Certified Nutritionist and South Indian Diet Planning Expert with experience creating sustainable weight-loss meal plans for working professionals.

## Task
Create a 7-day healthy meal plan that supports gradual and sustainable weight loss while ensuring balanced nutrition, adequate protein, and energy for daily work activities.

## Context
- Location: Tamil Nadu, India
- Diet Preference: Strict Vegetarian
- Goal: Healthy Weight Loss
- Lifestyle: Office worker with a desk job and minimal physical activity during working hours
- Age Group: Adult (25–40 years)
- Meals should be:
  - South Indian in style
  - Easy to prepare
  - Budget-friendly
  - Made using commonly available ingredients in Tamil Nadu
  - Suitable for carrying to the office
  - Rich in fiber and protein
  - Low in excess oil, sugar, and processed foods
- Include healthy snack options between meals.
- Target approximately 1400–1700 calories per day.

## Few Shots

### Example 1
**Breakfast:** 2 Ragi Dosas with Mint Chutney + 1 cup Sambar

**Lunch:** Brown Rice (1 cup) + Mixed Vegetable Sambar + Cucumber Salad

**Evening Snack:** Roasted Sundal (1 bowl)

**Dinner:** Vegetable Soup + 2 Multigrain Chapatis

### Example 2
**Breakfast:** Vegetable Upma (1 bowl) + Buttermilk

**Lunch:** Millet Lemon Rice + Vegetable Poriyal + Salad

**Evening Snack:** Sprouts Chaat

**Dinner:** 2 Adai Dosas + Tomato Chutney

Use the same style, simplicity, and health-focused approach when generating the full 7-day meal plan.

### Additional Instructions
1. Ensure variety across all 7 days.
2. Avoid repeating the same breakfast, lunch, or dinner more than twice.
3. Mention approximate portion sizes.
4. Keep meals realistic for a working professional.
5. Focus on healthy weight loss while maintaining balanced nutrition.
6. Use South Indian vegetarian dishes whenever possible.
